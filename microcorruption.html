Abstract

Background

The Microcorruption web debugger and the original 18 challenges were originally developed by Matasano in 2013. Matasano was later acquired by NCC Group, who rewrote the frontend and added 6 new challenges in 2022.

Why I did this

I wrote the exploits because I wanted respect; I wrote the writeups because otherwise nobody would understand the exploits. I published because this is something an AI could not do.

Evidence

I have done writeups for all 6 levels from NCC Group, as well as 3 from the original Matasano set.

  1. It would be impossible for an LLM to recycle content from existing writeups by other people, because in seven cases I was the first person to write one. This is verifiable with Wayback Machine snapshots and github.io commits.
    • I was first to do writeups on 4 of the 6 new challenges.
    • I was first to do writeups on novel exploit techniques for all three of the old challenges.
  2. I have OpenTimestamps on the exploits used in seven of the writeups. The LLMs that existed at the time could not have generated those exploits.
